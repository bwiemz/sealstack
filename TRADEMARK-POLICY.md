# SealStack trademark policy

This policy explains what you may and may not do with the "SealStack"
name, logo, and related marks. It complements — and is distinct from —
the Apache-2.0 license under which the source code is released.

The short version: **the code is open, the name is not**. That is the
standard posture for open-source projects that want to preserve a single
canonical distribution while letting anyone build on the software.

## 1. Ownership

The SealStack name and any SealStack logo or wordmark are owned by
Brandon Wiemers (the "Project Owner"). Even if the Project Owner has not
registered the marks with a trademark office, common-law rights apply
from first commercial use.

## 2. Permitted uses — no permission required

You may, without asking:

- **Factually describe compatibility or dependency.** "Works with
  SealStack." "Built on SealStack." "A SealStack connector." These are
  descriptive uses protected by nominative fair use.
- **Link to the official project.** Using the name in link text or
  URLs that point to `github.com/bwiemz/sealstack`, `sealstack.ai`, or
  official blog posts is fine.
- **Write tutorials, reviews, talks, or articles** about SealStack and
  refer to it by name. Mention it in your resume. Put it on a slide.
- **Fork the repository** under a different project name. The Apache-2.0
  license gives you the code; this policy asks that your fork not hold
  itself out as SealStack. Rename, re-logo, and attribute the origin.

## 3. Uses that require permission

These require written permission. Email **`trademark@sealstack.ai`** with
the details of your intended use:

- **Naming a product, service, company, domain, package, or app** that
  incorporates "SealStack" (e.g. "SealStack Cloud", "SealStackPro",
  "sealstack-enterprise" on npm / PyPI / crates.io).
- **Using the SealStack logo** as your own branding, on merchandise, or
  in a way that implies endorsement.
- **Any use likely to cause confusion** about whether your work is the
  official project, maintained by the Project Owner, or endorsed by the
  Project Owner.
- **Domain registrations** containing "sealstack" in a way that could be
  mistaken for the official domain.

## 4. Attribution requirements

If you redistribute SealStack source code, binaries, or derivative works
under Apache-2.0, you must preserve the `LICENSE`, `NOTICE`, and this
`TRADEMARK-POLICY.md` alongside the source. Dropping attribution is a
license violation regardless of trademark considerations.

If you operate a hosted service that uses SealStack, attribution is
appreciated but not required — "Powered by SealStack" with a link back
is the friendly-neighbor convention.

## 5. Reporting misuse

If you encounter a product or project using the SealStack name or logo
in a way this policy forbids, email **`trademark@sealstack.ai`** with
the specifics (URL, screenshots, package name, etc.). Misuse that the
Project Owner does not act on quickly is not evidence of permission —
the Project Owner expressly reserves all rights and enforcement
discretion.

## 6. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated as the project evolves. Material changes will
be noted in the `CHANGELOG.md`. Your permission to use the marks under
previous versions of this policy continues for uses you began in good
faith under those versions.

## 7. Contact

For questions about trademark use or permission requests:
**`trademark@sealstack.ai`**.

For everything else — bug reports, feature ideas, general inquiries —
